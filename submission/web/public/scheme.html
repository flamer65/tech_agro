Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) is a government scheme that aims to provide financial assistance to small and marginal farmers in India. The scheme was launched by the Government of India in February 2019 with the objective of ensuring the livelihood security of farmers and to augment their income.

Benefits:

  1. Financial assistance: Under the scheme, eligible farmers receive Rs. 6,000 per year in three equal installments of Rs. 2,000 each. The assistance is provided directly to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) mode.
  2. Income: The financial assistance provided under the scheme can help farmers in meeting their agricultural requirements and can contribute to the improvement of their income.
  3. Security: The scheme can provide a safety net to small and marginal farmers who often face economic uncertainty due to fluctuations in agricultural production and prices.
  4. Incentive for cultivation: The financial assistance can act as an incentive for farmers to cultivate their land and to invest in agricultural inputs and technology.

Eligibilty:

  1. Land ownership: The scheme is applicable to small and marginal farmers who own cultivable land of up to 2 hectares.
  2. Citizenship: The farmer must be a citizen of India.
  3. Age: There is no age limit for beneficiaries of the scheme.
  4. Bank account: The farmer must have an active bank account.
  5. Exclusion: The scheme excludes institutional landholders, farmers who have retired from government service, and those who pay income tax.

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is an agricultural insurance scheme launched by the Government of India in 2016. The primary objective of PMFBY is to provide financial support to farmers in the event of crop failure or damage due to natural calamities, pests, or diseases.


The scheme aims to ensure that farmers get maximum financial benefit for the premium paid by them for the insurance cover. It also aims to provide financial stability to farmers, as crop failure can often lead to a significant loss of income and can push them into debt.
PMFBY covers all farmers, including sharecroppers and tenant farmers. It provides comprehensive coverage against yield losses, crop damage, and localized calamities such as hailstorms, floods, droughts, etc.

Benefits:

  1. Financial Protection: PMFBY provides farmers with financial protection against crop losses due to natural calamities or unforeseen events. This helps farmers avoid financial distress and remain financially stable.
  2. Affordable Premiums: The premium rates for PMFBY are kept affordable for farmers, with the government subsidizing a significant portion of the premium cost.
  3. Comprehensive Coverage: PMFBY provides comprehensive coverage against yield losses, crop damage, and localized calamities such as hailstorms, floods, droughts, etc. This helps farmers cover all possible risks and avoid any unforeseen financial burden.
  4. Timely Payouts: PMFBY ensures timely payouts to farmers in the event of crop losses. This helps farmers get back on their feet quickly and continue with their farming activities.

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) is an irrigation scheme launched by the Government of India in 2015. The primary objective of PMKSY is to enhance the irrigation potential and improve water use efficiency in agriculture, thereby increasing the productivity of crops.
PMKSY aims to bring all farmers under the ambit of irrigation and ensure that every agricultural field gets adequate water supply for its irrigation needs. It aims to improve water use efficiency in agriculture, thereby ensuring sustainable use of water resources.

Benfits:

  1. Increased Irrigation Coverage: PMKSY aims to bring all farmers under the ambit of irrigation and ensure that every agricultural field gets adequate water supply for its irrigation needs. This will lead to increased irrigation coverage and help farmers cultivate more crops.
  2. Improved Water Use Efficiency: PMKSY aims to improve water use efficiency in agriculture by promoting the use of efficient irrigation technologies such as micro-irrigation and drip irrigation. This will help farmers use water more efficiently and sustainably.
  3. Productivity Improvement: PMKSY aims to increase the productivity of crops by providing adequate water supply for their irrigation needs. This will lead to increased crop yields and better incomes for farmers.
  4. Crop Diversification: PMKSY aims to encourage crop diversification by promoting the cultivation of crops that require less water and are more suitable for the local agro-climatic conditions. This will help farmers diversify their income sources and reduce their dependence on a single crop.

Ayushman Sahakar Scheme is a scheme launched by the Government of India in 2020 to promote the development of healthcare infrastructure by supporting cooperative organizations. The primary objective of the scheme is to provide affordable and quality healthcare services to people in rural areas through cooperative-based models.
The scheme aims to establish a strong network of healthcare cooperatives that can provide quality healthcare services to people in rural areas at affordable prices. It aims to support the development of healthcare infrastructure and encourage the participation of community-based organizations in the healthcare sector.

Benefits:

  1. Affordable Healthcare: Ayushman Sahakar Scheme aims to provide affordable healthcare services to people in rural areas by supporting cooperative-based models. This will help people access quality healthcare services without having to bear high costs.
  2. Improved Healthcare Infrastructure: The scheme aims to support the development of healthcare infrastructure by providing financial assistance to cooperative organizations. This will help improve the availability and quality of healthcare services in rural areas.
  3. Community Participation: Ayushman Sahakar Scheme encourages the participation of community-based organizations in the healthcare sector. This will help create a sense of ownership and responsibility among the local communities towards healthcare services.
  4. Employment Opportunities: The scheme will generate employment opportunities in the healthcare sector by supporting the development of healthcare cooperatives. This will help create jobs for local people and improve their livelihoods.
